## Indian Market Context (Baseline — March 2026)

### Interest Rate Environment
- RBI repo rate: ~6.25% (in a cutting cycle from 6.5% peak in 2023-24)
- G-Sec 10-year yield: ~6.8-7.0%
- RBI stance: Accommodative with inflation watch — CPI targeting 4% band
- Impact: Rate-sensitive sectors (NBFCs, real estate, auto) benefit; margins for leveraged cos improving

### INR/USD Dynamics
- INR range: 83-87 vs USD (depreciation trend, ~2-3% annual)
- Impact: Export-oriented cos (IT, Pharma, Textiles) benefit; import-heavy cos (Chemicals, Electronics) face headwind
- RBI intervenes to limit excessive volatility

### India Macro Regime (FY2025-26)
- GDP growth: 6.5-7% nominal; real ~6.0%
- Capex cycle: Government-led infra capex continuing (roads, railways, defence)
- Private capex: Recovering, led by PLI-linked manufacturing and data centers
- Consumer demand: Urban slowing, rural recovering (normal monsoon FY2025)
- Credit growth: ~13-15% YoY; NBFCs growing faster than banks

### Key Sector Observations
- **IT Services**: Demand recovery from US client caution; AI/GenAI investments driving new deals
- **Pharmaceuticals**: US generics pricing stabilising; domestic formulations growing ~10%
- **Auto**: Two-wheeler recovery led by rural; EV transition risk for ICE-heavy cos
- **Cement**: Capacity addition peaking; pricing under pressure in South India
- **Specialty Chemicals**: China oversupply headwind persists; import substitution theme intact
- **FMCG**: Volume growth recovering; rural normalization; valuations remain stretched (40-50x PE)
- **Banks**: NIM compression as rates ease; asset quality stable; credit costs normalised
- **Real Estate**: Super-cycle continues in metros; mid-income housing strong; affordable housing subdued

### Valuation Context
- Nifty 50 PE: ~22-24x (near long-term average)
- Midcap PE premium: ~15-20% over largecap — elevated but not extreme
- Smallcap: Selected pockets overvalued; sector rotation ongoing
- Quality premium: High-ROCE, low-debt cos trade at significant premium — scrutinise carefully

### Geopolitical Watch
- US-China trade tensions: Benefiting India in PLI sectors (electronics, chemicals)
- China dumping risk: Steel, chemicals, solar panels — monitor for Indian cos
- Middle East: Oil price volatility; India imports ~85% crude — every $10/bbl = ~0.3% GDP impact
- US reciprocal tariffs (2025): IT services largely unaffected; goods exporters watching closely
